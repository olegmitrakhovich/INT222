World Capitals
A capital city is usually defined by the country's government offices. A capital city can be specified by law or constitution, by the government of the country it is located in. This city is best used for government's central meetings. Most of the country's leaders would work in the capital city, making it an important location.

France                                                                                                                                                                                    Paris
Paris, France's capital, is a major European city and a global center for art, fashion and culture. Most known for its landmarks such as the Eiffel Tower and the Gothic Notre-Dame cathedral.

Russia                                                                                                                                                                                 Moscow
Moscow, located on the Moskva River in western Russia. In the core of the city is the Kremlin a complex where the country's president resides. The city is also known for such landmarks as the Red Square, known to some as the country's center.

United States of America                                                                                                                                            Washington, DC
Washington, DC, the U.S. capital, located on the Potomac River. Best known for its imposing neoclassical monuments and buildings. Home to the three branches of the federal government: the Capitol, White House and Supreme Court.
